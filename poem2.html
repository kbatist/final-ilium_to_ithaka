Poem Two
"Odysseus' Yearning"

"Odysseus, sail home—" "The sea keeps his secrets, salt-stained and endless, yet Odysseus leans toward Ithaca as though memory itself were a compass. Every wave whispers of Penelope, her loom weaving patience into years, her silence louder than the sirens.

"Return to me," the winds seem to call, He is a man caught in the history of man— a tale of wars and wanderings, of choices carved into stone, yet soft with yearning.

The gods write destinies in thunder, but he writes his own in longing, each exile a stanza, each return a prayer. He remembers the song of home, fragile as breath, and wonders if love can outlast the myths that bind him. What is a hero, if not a man who aches to belong, who carries the weight of stories but dreams only of her hands"
